    <h4> Details </h4>
    <!-- [Goal] for [purpose] by [method] <br> -->
    I'm in the process of building an elevated desk in order to have the option to sit or stand. Learning wood working as I go and using mis-stored lumber from around the house means it will  not be as high quality as I would prefer, but if I don't like it I can just make another one. <br>
    <!-- [Progress], [Lessons learned] -->
    So far I've gotten the hang of various hand- and machine- powered woodshop tools, inlcuding not limited to the; chisel, file, circular saw, hand saw, band saw, table saw, drill press, belt, palm, and block sanders, non destructive tools such as the square, tape measure, and level, as well as techniques for cutting, cleaning, joining, sanding, and finishing various types of hard and soft wood. In order to learn and practice this much before making a proper desk, I crafted a mini "desktop desk" that will raise my monitors to a more comfortable level. Despite opting for pockethole joints on the proper desk I chose to make this with mortise and tenon joints, mostly for fun TODO <br>
    Beyond making myself a desk this project has been and will continue to be an enriching experience in learning how to work wood.
